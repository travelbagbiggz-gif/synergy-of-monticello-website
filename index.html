TMS Therapy: Hope When Medication Hasn't Worked

Transcranial Magnetic Stimulation (TMS) is a non-invasive, FDA-cleared treatment for major depression. It uses targeted magnetic pulses to stimulate underactive areas of the brain — without medication side effects.

Our NeuroStar TMS program is led by experienced clinicians and is covered by most major insurance plans. Each session takes about 20 minutes; a full course is typically 5 days a week for 6 weeks.

  • No anesthesia, no medication, no downtime
  • Drive yourself to and from every session
  • 58% remission rate — more than 1 in 2 patients
  • Covered by most major insurance plans
  • FDA-cleared and clinically proven since 2008
  • Over 4.5 million treatments performed worldwide

How TMS Works

A specialized coil is positioned over your head. Magnetic pulses pass painlessly through the skull, stimulating neurons in the prefrontal cortex — the area of the brain that regulates mood. No sedation required. Resume daily activities immediately after.

Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

Are you accepting new patients?+
Yes! We are currently accepting new patients across all service lines — psychiatry, therapy, and TMS. Call (845) 791-8800 or use our online scheduler to book your intake appointment.
Do you offer telehealth appointments?+
Yes. Several of our providers offer telehealth appointments via secure video. Christine Aranyi, NP specifically highlights telehealth availability. Ask our front desk when scheduling.
How long does TMS therapy take?+
A standard TMS course is 5 sessions per week for 6 weeks (30 sessions total). Each session is approximately 20 minutes. You drive yourself — no anesthesia or recovery time needed. Some patients begin to see improvement within the first few weeks.
Is TMS covered by insurance?+
TMS is covered by Medicare, Medicaid, and most major commercial insurance plans when you meet medical necessity criteria (typically having tried 1–2 antidepressants without adequate response). We handle the prior authorization process for you. Call us to verify your specific coverage.
What should I bring to my first appointment?+
Please bring: a valid photo ID, your insurance card, a list of current medications and dosages, any prior psychiatric records or lab results, and a list of questions or concerns you'd like to discuss. Arrive 15 minutes early to complete intake paperwork.
Do you treat children and adolescents?+
Yes. Dr. Thandar Win is board-certified in both general and child/adolescent psychiatry. Laurie Glasgow, NP treats children through adults. Several of our therapists also specialize in working with younger patients.
What is your cancellation policy?+
We ask for at least 24 hours notice for cancellations or rescheduling. Late cancellations or no-shows may be subject to a fee. Please call (845) 791-8800 as soon as possible if you need to change your appointment.
Do you offer services in Spanish?+
Yes. Mary Kay Dollard, FNP is fully fluent in Spanish and American Sign Language. We are committed to making care accessible to all members of our diverse community.
